Packaging in a circular economy
The linear packaging industry
Packaging: Benefits and Challenges
Challenges: when it becomes a litter or even in the plastic soup.
The value of the empty packaging is maybe less than a cent.
Cleaning up is a large negative cost.
More expensive to clean up the ocean.
Benefits: protect the product, keep food fresh, save food waste, give you information about the product and seduce consumers to buy it.
Designers should not only think about the design, but also the whole cycle.
We should design a product that lasts longer, or maybe you don’t need disposables.
linear model (of take, make, use and dispose) is destroying the material value embedded in (waste) packaging.

Benefits of Packaging in Detail
Product protection, utility and communication.

Challenges for Plastic Packaging
A large amount of our packaging waste consists of plastics.
1.Plastics waste generation is growing rapidly, negatively affecting the environment and human health.
2.Recovering plastic waste represents a substantial economic opportunity.
3.Currently only a limited amount of global plastics waste (most of it packaging) is recovered.

A circular perspective for packaging
Packaging and the Circular Economy
Packaging is considered a Fast Moving Consumer Good (FMCG). These are products that are designed to have a short lifespan and are constantly in rotation.
Products are consumed in large quantities.
Strategies:
The first design strategy: rethink : reimagining the design or application of the packaging (or product) in order to maximise positive (and minimise negative) environmental and societal impacts.
Why are we doing this?
Why do we use this packaging?
Why are we using disposables?
The second one: reuse: collecting, cleaning and reusing packaging items for the same purpose.
We have to organize a system to do that.(combination systems)
The third one: recycling: converting waste packaging into materials for new packaging items.
The last one: renew: using renewable and/or biodegradable materials in packaging.
Recycle again or make it biodegradable.

The Packaging Lifecycle
linear economy: resources make a one-way trip from raw material through the production and use phases to become waste.

Developing Circular Product-Packaging Combinations
Whenever a product depends on its packaging in order to be used they are considered a Product-Packaging Combination (PPC).
Developing Product-Packaging Combinations (PPCs):
1.Integrate the development of the product and its packaging.( Develop the packaging while you are designing the product.)
2.Consider sustainability aspects at the beginning of a new PPC development process.
3.Involve all stakeholders that are part of the PPC’s value chain.
