Linear or nonlinear relationship.
In a linear relationship, increasing one item always increases or decreases another at the same rate.
Increasing one factor may increase or decrease a connected factor, depending on the situation.
nonlinear approaches are necessary to understand infectious diseases in a changing climate.(reason: ecological systems are complex and they involve lots of interactions, which often end up being nonlinear because they involve things like desity dependence, competition that have some optimum that might turn over over time).
