Green house gas
The Role of the Atmosphere
Some solar radiation is radiated back towards space as long wave infrared radiation.
Green house gases absorb outgoing long-wave radiation and transmit it back towards the earth’s surface.
the temperature of our planet is most substantially influenced by the amount of greenhouse gas in the atmosphere.
CO2 and temperature
carbon dioxide levels have paralleled temperature.
Humanity has become the major force changing our planet in this time.
Sources of Greenhouse Gases
By burning fossil fuels: coal, natural gas, and oil. Coal is the largest contributor.
agriculture, mining, and forestry
Where is the heat?
Most of the warming that greenhouse gases have added to our planet have gone into the oceans. As water warms, it expands and that makes sea levels rise.
The same amount of heat energy creates a different temperature change in different materials
Temperature Rise
the northern parts of the planet are warming many times faster than the tropics.
Floods and droughts
Warmer air holds more water. And when that air with more moisture is ready to rain (or snow), it does so in greater amounts.
More precipitation, more climate change.
Warmer temperatures cause more evaporation and drying of land.
The Direct Effects of CO2
Ocean Acidification
Saltwater typically has a pH between 7.5 and 8.4, which makes it very slightly alkaline.
When the green gas gets absorbed, it produces carbonic acid, which makes the world’s oceans more acidic.
promote the breakdown of calcium carbonate（form the skeletons of animals）
Carbon dioxide effects on plants
plants change in ways that have consequences for health.
Human Behavior
3 ways can reduce the climate change on human beings
1.Through adaptation policies and actions to protect health from climate impacts (the black area in the graph below).
2.Through mitigation, such as reducing emissions in the first place (the dark blue area).
3.Through health co-benefits - reaping health gains from climate-friendly policies and individual behavior (the light blue area).
Adaptation is changing to deal with a warmer world and mitigation is preventing that warming.
