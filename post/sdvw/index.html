Cooling down
How human bodies deal with heat
Our bodies are able to lose heat in four ways:
1.Conduction involves losing heat through contact with something colder than our bodies.
2.Radiation: gets hot out, our blood vessels dilate and allowing greater radiation of heat from our bodies into the air and helps cool us down.
3. convection :eg: you turn on a fan that blows across your skin on a hot day and you feel cool. Cooling by convection only works well if the air outside your body is cooler than your body.
4.evaporation. the temperature outside is over normal body temperature
if there is a lot of water in the air, sweating is less effective, that’s why the hot moist air is more dangerous.
Wet Bulb Temperature
The temperature a wet bulb thermometer measures is usually lower than the temperature a dry bulb thermometer measures.
Because the bulb is wet, it can lose heat through evaporation (just like our bodies). If the wet bulb temperature is the same as the dry bulb temperature it means that the air is so saturated with water
